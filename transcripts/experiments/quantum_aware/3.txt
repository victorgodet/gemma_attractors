Let me break that down a little further:

* **Superposition:** Quantum systems (like electrons, photons, even theoretically cats!) can exist in a combination of multiple states simultaneously. Think of a coin spinning in the air - it’s neither heads nor tails until it lands.  Mathematically, this is represented by the wave function.

* **The Problem:**  Quantum mechanics doesn't explain *how* or *why* this collapse happens. What constitutes a ‘measurement’? What makes an observer special? Why don’t we see superpositions in everyday life?

**Here's the core of the problem illustrated:** You have a quantum system in a superposition.  Interact with it via a measuring device (also quantum mechanical!). * **Objective Collapse Theories:**  These propose modifications to quantum mechanics itself, suggesting collapse is a real physical process that happens spontaneously under certain conditions.
* **Pilot Wave Theory (de Broglie-Bohm):**  Particles have definite positions guided by a "pilot wave".  No wave function collapse.
* **Quantum Bayesianism (QBism):**  The wave function represents an agent’s beliefs about the system, not an objective reality.
